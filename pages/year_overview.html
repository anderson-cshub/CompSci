Paper 1 Overview

Covers programming languages, databases, networks, data types, legislation, ethical issues, data structures, software development, Boolean algebra and programming fundamentals.

Covers computational thinking, applied computational methods, algorithm implementation, recursion, trees, graphs, queues, stacks, data mining, performance modelling, pipelining, visualisation, OOP, optimisation algorithms and Big O notation.

Year Overview – Upper 6th

The long‑term plan for Upper 6th Computing covers both Paper 1 and Paper 2 content across Michaelmas, Lent and Trinity terms, with NEA work integrated throughout the year.

Paper 1 Overview

Michaelmas Term

Completion of programming languages content, introduction to databases, data types, networks, and continued NEA development.

Lent Term

Computer‑related legislation, ethical and cultural issues, revision of data structures and software development, alongside ongoing NEA work.

Trinity Term

Boolean algebra revision, programming fundamentals revision, targeted exam preparation, and final NEA completion.

Paper 2 Overview

Michaelmas Term

Computational thinking, applied computational methods, algorithm implementation, recursion, stacks, queues, graphs, arrays, records, lists, tuples, and NEA integration.

Lent Term

Tree traversal (pre‑order, in‑order, post‑order), data mining, performance modelling, pipelining, visualisation, object‑oriented programming, and continued NEA work.

Trinity Term

Adjacency matrices and lists, Dijkstra’s algorithm, A* algorithm, efficiency analysis, Big O notation, and exam technique.
